🏛️ U.S. Congress — 1st Congressional District (PA-01)

Bob Harvie — Falls Township

Bucks County Commissioner who announced his congressional bid in April 2025. Lifelong Bucks County resident born in Bristol Borough, graduated magna cum laude from George Washington University, spent 20 years as a teacher and Social Studies Department chair at Bucks County Technical High School. Elected County Commissioner in 2019, named board chair in 2022, re-elected in 2023. Secured endorsements from U.S. Reps. Madeleine Dean, Chris Deluzio, and Mary Gay Scanlon, plus the Bucks and Montgomery County Democratic Committees. Campaign centers on economic fairness and kitchen-table issues.

Lucia Simonelli — Quakertown Borough

Holds a PhD in mathematics and has served as a scientific advisor in the U.S. Senate on energy and climate policy. Born in Lansdale, now settled in Quakertown. Backed by multiple Indivisible chapters and former state Rep. Helen Tai. Emerged as Harvie's sole challenger after the March petition deadline.

Bucks County Beacon — Democratic State House Candidate Kristin Egan Goes on Record to Oppose Controversial US 1 Project in Lower Bucks County (April 2, 2026)

Egan became the first state office candidate to formally oppose the RC3 project, a $227 million PennDOT plan to reconstruct a four-mile stretch of US Route 1 through Langhorne Manor Borough and Middletown Township, including a controversial cloverleaf interchange and removal of parallel service roads. She cited "safety concerns, environmental risks, and impacts on quality of life," and pledged to be "the voice in Harrisburg that demands PennDOT respects our residents." A public information meeting on the project is scheduled for April 16 in Langhorne. Local elections in November saw eight of nine affected council and supervisor seats go to candidates opposing the current plan.
