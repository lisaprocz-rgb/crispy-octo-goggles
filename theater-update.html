📰 Broadway News & Buzz
🏆 Tony Awards
79th Annual Tony Awards: June 7, 2026 at Radio City Music Hall
Broadcast live on CBS and streaming on Paramount+, 8–11 PM ET. Returns to Radio City for the first time in years.
🏆 Tony Awards
Nominations Announced May 5 · Eligibility Cutoff April 26
The 2025–26 eligibility window runs April 28, 2025 through April 26, 2026. With the cutoff just days away, the Tony race is in full swing.
🏆 Tony Awards — Best Musical Frontrunners
The Lost Boys, TITANÍQUE, and Two Strangers (Carry a Cake Across New York) lead the pack
Three very different musicals are generating the most awards heat in the Best Musical category this season.
🏆 Tony Awards — Best Revival of a Play
Nathan Lane early favorite for Death of a Salesman; stacked field includes Joe Turner's Come and Gone and Proof
With Ayo Edebiri in Proof and Taraji P. Henson in Joe Turner's Come and Gone also entering the picture, Best Revival of a Play is one of the most competitive categories of the season.
🏆 Tony Awards — Best Revival of a Musical
Cats: The Jellicle Ball is an immediate frontrunner; Ragtime, Chess, and Rocky Horror Show all contending
The drag ballroom reimagining of Cats has generated enormous buzz, but rarely-seen revivals of Ragtime, Chess, and the incoming Rocky Horror Show make this an epic showdown.
🏆 Broadway Buzz
Inaugural Broadway Ensemble Awards held April 6, 2026
The first-ever Broadway Ensemble Awards celebrated unsung heroes of the stage. Chess and Ragtime were among the big winners of the inaugural ceremony.
🏆 Broadway Buzz
Broadway Spring 2026 brings a record-setting wave of star power
This spring's Broadway openings feature an extraordinary concentration of stars: Nathan Lane, Laurie Metcalf, Ayo Edebiri, Don Cheadle, Kelli O'Hara, Rose Byrne, Taraji P. Henson, Adrien Brody, and Tessa Thompson, among others.
🎭 Casting
Megan Thee Stallion makes history in Moulin Rouge!
The Grammy-winning rapper/singer is making her Broadway debut as Zidler in Moulin Rouge! The Musical (through May 17), becoming the first female-identifying performer to play the role in any production worldwide. She was briefly hospitalized with exhaustion and dehydration during a performance in April.
🎭 Casting
Adrien Brody and Tessa Thompson make their Broadway debuts in The Fear of 13
The two-time Oscar winner and Golden Globe nominee star in the true-crime drama about Nick Yarris, a man who spent 22 years on death row. Official opening is April 15 at the James Earl Jones Theatre.
🎭 Casting
J. Harrison Ghee leads new Hadestown principal cast as Hermes
The Tony and Grammy winner joined the long-running hit in March alongside Joshua Colley (Orpheus), Jordan Tyson (Eurydice), Gary Dourdan (Hades, Broadway debut), and Gaby Moreno (Persephone, Broadway debut).
🎭 Casting
Dylan Mulvaney and Abigail Barlow join Six on Broadway
TikTok/Broadway star Dylan Mulvaney took on the role of Anne Boleyn from February 16. Abigail Barlow (The Unofficial Bridgerton Musical) joined as Katherine Howard.
🎭 Casting
Mark Ballas returns to Chicago as Billy Flynn through May 3
The Dancing with the Stars veteran reunites with former dance partner Whitney Leavitt (Roxie Hart) at the Ambassador Theatre in a limited engagement.
🎭 Casting
Keri René Fuller takes over as Elphaba in Wicked
Fuller assumed the role full time beginning March 3. Emma Flynn (Cher in West End's Clueless) is making her Broadway debut as Glinda.
🎭 Closing Notice
Daniel Radcliffe plays his final performance in Every Brilliant Thing on May 24
The Tony winner's limited run in the solo show is ending; the production itself closes June 28.
🎭 Closing Notice
Lea Michele's final Chess performance is June 21
The star will take her final bow in the revival of the ABBA-adjacent musical on June 21.

🔭 Industry News
Broadway union contracts ratified through 2028 — strike averted
After tense negotiations in fall 2025, both AFM Local 802 (musicians) and Actors' Equity reached agreements with the Broadway League. The musicians' contract runs through August 2028. What would have been Broadway's first strike since 2007 was averted.
